# Problem Statement

## Current Challenges for Docusign Sales Teams

Sales teams at Docusign currently face a time-consuming and manual process when preparing for strategic account conversations. Before engaging with a large enterprise customer, account executives must independently research the company using a variety of sources—including public filings, news coverage, LinkedIn, internal CRM data, and usage reports. Synthesizing this information into a coherent narrative that articulates Docusign’s value, identifies gaps, and highlights growth opportunities requires significant effort.

This preparation often takes hours per account and leads to inconsistent results. While some representatives conduct in-depth research, others may provide minimal analysis. The outcome is variable deal quality, missed opportunities for account expansion, and conversations that may fail to resonate at the executive level.

Although the bookscrub sheet contains extensive internal data (such as product adoption, engagement metrics, workflow coverage, and renewal health), this valuable information remains locked within a spreadsheet and is rarely converted into a persuasive strategic narrative.

## The Proposed Solution

This tool automates the entire account preparation process. Given a company name, it ingests the internal usage data and conducts five sequential large language model (LLM) research calls to generate a comprehensive profile of the target company—including business overview, organizational structure, agreement landscape, and commercial footprint. The results are then assembled into a professionally formatted Google Doc with nine structured sections, ready for use in executive briefings or account planning sessions. What previously required hours of manual work is now accomplished in minutes, ensuring consistent depth and quality across every account.
