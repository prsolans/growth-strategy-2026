Growth Strategy Generator

Automated account preparation for enterprise sales — powered by internal data and LLM research
Docusign Value Consulting   ·   March 2026   ·   Confidential — Internal Use Only

The Problem

Preparing for a strategic enterprise account conversation at Docusign is a manual, time-intensive process. Before meeting a large customer, account executives must independently research the company across fragmented sources — public filings, news, LinkedIn, internal CRM, usage reports — and synthesize that into a coherent narrative that articulates Docusign value, identifies gaps, and surfaces expansion opportunities.

The result is inconsistency at scale. Research depth varies by rep. Internal usage data sits locked in a spreadsheet and rarely becomes a strategic narrative. Conversations miss executive-level resonance, and expansion opportunities go unrecognized.

The Solution

The Growth Strategy Generator automates account preparation end-to-end. Given a company name, it reads internal usage data from the bookscrub sheet, runs five sequential LLM research calls against an internal endpoint (GPT-4o with Bing Grounding), and assembles a professionally formatted, nine-section Google Doc ready for executive briefings or account planning sessions.

Recommendations are grounded in a deterministic signal-matching engine that evaluates 15 Docusign products and 4 bundle configurations against actual customer usage data. The LLM layers business context on top of those signals — every recommendation can be traced to a specific usage threshold or account behavior.

Data-Driven Recommendations — Signal Matching Engine

Before any LLM call runs, a deterministic signal-matching engine evaluates 15 Docusign products and 4 bundle configurations against 60+ bookscrub fields using explicit thresholds. Each product receives a strength rating (Strong, Moderate, Exploratory) and a status (Recommended, In Use, Not Relevant). This output is injected into the LLM system prompt for Call 5, so the Priority Map is grounded in account behavior — not generated from general knowledge. Products already in use are explicitly excluded from “recommended” status, and accounts in regulated industries automatically receive boosted recommendations for IDV, SMS Auth, and Agreement Desk.

LLM Pipeline — 5 Sequential Calls

Each call receives the output of all prior calls as context, building a progressively richer picture of the account.

1
Account Profile No prior context
Company overview · ~5 business units · financials · SWOT · ≥5 executives (CLO, CPO, CFO, CIO, CTO) · tech stack · strategic initiatives
2
Business Map Uses: Call 1
Full org hierarchy (BU → Dept → Function, 40+ nodes) · agreement intensity rating per node · Corporate / Shared Services always included
3
Agreement Landscape Uses: Calls 1 + 2
Top 20 agreement types scored by volume + complexity · categorized and ranked · if LLM fails: simplified retry, then deterministic generation from industry config
4
Contract Commerce Uses: Calls 1 + 3
$ estimates for commerce flowing through agreements · broken down by department and agreement type · commercial relationship counts (employees, suppliers, customers)
5
Priority Map — Synthesis All calls + internal signals
5–7 company priorities mapped to Docusign capabilities · expansion opportunities · action plan · grounded in signal-matched product recommendations injected into system prompt

Resilience & Error Handling

  • Per-call isolation: Any failed call returns {}; the doc renders that section with a graceful fallback and execution continues
  • JSON retry: Malformed LLM responses trigger an automatic retry with an explicit “provide valid JSON” prompt
  • Call 3 fallback chain: Primary (20 types) → simplified retry (15 types) → deterministic generation from industry config tables if both fail
  • Source filtering: Hallucinated or placeholder URLs are blocked before the Sources section renders
